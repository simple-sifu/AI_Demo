# Learning Theory

An outline of ideas and practices for learning how to learn — focused on improving the **process**, not just chasing outcomes.

---

## Growth Mindset

Focus on improving the process, not the outcomes.

- Outcomes are feedback, not the goal — use them to refine how you learn
- When results fall short, ask: *What part of my process broke down?*
- Celebrate effort, strategy, and iteration — not just grades or completion
- Replace “I’m bad at this” with “I haven’t built the right process yet”

---

## Time Management

Use tools like **Google Calendar** to plan, track, and evaluate how you spend learning time.

- Block time for specific tasks before the day begins
- At the end of each day/week, review: *Did I do what I planned?*
- For items not done, ask: *Why not?* (too ambitious, wrong priority, distraction, unclear next step?)
- Use those answers to improve the **next** plan — perfect the process, not just the schedule
- Track patterns over time: when do you consistently over- or under-estimate?

---

## Deep Processing

Go beyond surface memorization — understand how ideas connect.

- Ask: *How is this idea related to what I already know?*
- Ask: *What is the most important idea here?* — distill to the core
- Compare and contrast: similarities, differences, cause and effect
- Teach it back in your own words — if you can’t explain it simply, you haven’t processed it deeply

---

## Stories & Visuals

Use narrative and imagery to make ideas stick.

- Turn abstract concepts into **stories** — characters, conflict, resolution
- **Draw pictures** or diagrams — even rough sketches force you to structure the idea
- Create mental scenes: “Imagine this concept as a…”
- Link new material to vivid, personal examples you won’t forget

---

## Note Taking

Capture and organize what you learn in a way that supports review and deep processing.

- *What method?* (Cornell, outline, mind map, digital vs. paper)
- *When to take notes?* (during lecture, after reading, while coding)
- Notes should help you **reconstruct** the idea later — not just copy verbatim
- Review and refine notes: highlight the most important idea, add connections
- Link notes to stories, visuals, and calendar blocks for spaced review
